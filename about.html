About

What is Litter Visualizer's Purpose?

Litter Visualizer was made to help visualize the trash collection done during cleanup events around Prince George's County. The map displays where different types of litter was collected around PG County, while the chart shows the total amount of litter bags collected at different types of locations (e.g. Council District, DPWT Main District, Watershed). By using the map, users can see what types of litter frequently appear in a specific area, which can help future cleanup entities better prepare for dealing with unique items in those areas. The chart will show which areas have the most / least bags of litter, similarly allowing cleanup entities to prepare for specific loads and to infer which areas are in greater need of cleanup events.

Where is the data from?

The visualizer uses the LitterTRAK API from the Prince George's County Open Data Website.

How to Use

To see where different types of litter are located, use the map filter located to the right of the map. Click the dropdown menu under the label "Type of Litter" to pick a specific type of litter you want to search for. Then, click your chosen option and 30 locations with that type of litter will appear on the map. To reset the visualization back to the default option (Bags of Garbage), press the green "Refresh" button under the "Type of Litter" dropdown menu.

To see how many bags of litter there are based on location, use the chart filter located to the right of the chart. Click the dropdown menu under the label "X-axis Categories" to pick either Council District, DPWT Main District, or Major Watershed as the x-axis category for the chart. Then, click your chosen location and the chart will automatically update the x-axis to show the categories within that location type. To reset the visualization back to the default option (Council District), press the purple "Refresh" button under the "X-axis Categories" dropdown menu.
